About

Drawing inspiration from Zyte's books.toscrape.com, we offer a dynamic platform that mimics the dynamics of the music industry. You can use our website and API to learn how to scrape. Built by Tilburg Science Hub for educational purposes, we hope this platform helps students and data science enthusiasts around the world to learn scraping without running into (legal) issues.

Exploring Boundaries

We've simulated the behavior of hundreds of users, listening to thousands of different songs and artists. The data, initialized with a list of artists and songs retrieved from Mahkaila's GitHub repository and simulated in R encompasses artists of different degrees of popularity and users that develop preferences for these artists and songs, producing a captivating array of consumption behaviors.

Explore popular or featured artists, discover emerging trends, and understand user preferences as you delve into the interplay between music and data. Our platform also presents you with a comprehensive API, opening doors to endless possibilities for analysis, visualization, and innovation.

Join the Open Source Movement

We believe in the power of collaboration and learning through hands-on experience. Our project is entirely open source, and we invite you to become part of our journey. Contribute, experiment, and shape how the platform looks and feels or how (realistic) the data is simulated. Explore our repository at github.com/tilburgsciencehub/music-to-scrape and make your mark.

Your Privacy Matters

As advocates of responsible data usage, we take your privacy seriously. We've outlined our privacy practices and usage terms in our dedicated Privacy & Terms section. Feel free to explore this section to understand how we handle data, ensure your security, and create a safe environment for exploration.
